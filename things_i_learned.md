- 01 Jan 2025
  - [Different and similar perceptions of communication among software developers](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0950584925000370) ([RDEL summary](https://rdel.substack.com/p/rdel-123-how-differently-do-software))
    - Developers often interpret the same written messages differently. This makes everyday communication another source of friction in teams.
    - Differences are driven by communication style preferences (tone, formality, emotional cues), not experience or demographics.
    - Casual elements like emojis or informal phrasing can help some developers and annoy or confuse others.
    - Making communication norms explicit helps reduce friction more than expecting people to adapt implicitly.
